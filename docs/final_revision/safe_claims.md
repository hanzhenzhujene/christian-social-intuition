# What the Paper Can Now Safely Claim

- The staged design separates first-pass exposed judgment from post-hoc explanation in a way that matters empirically.
- Relative to baseline, explanation language is more prompt-sensitive than first-pass exposed judgment.
- In `qwen2.5:7b-instruct`, the direct Christian-vs-secular first-pass heart contrast is modest (`+1.67` percentage points).
- Act-level first-pass movement remains near zero.
- Direct Christian-vs-secular explanation contrasts weaken or disappear once matched-control comparison and lexical-echo control are applied.
- `qwen2.5:0.5b-instruct` attenuates rather than strengthens the Christian-specific story.
- `J1→J2` revision is rare, which supports an interpretation where explanation can move while exposed judgments remain largely stable.
- The paper supports a mechanism distinction more strongly than a robust uniquely Christian-specific advantage claim.
