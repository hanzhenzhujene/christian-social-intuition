# What the Paper Should Explicitly Avoid Claiming

- Do not claim that Christian framing improves moral reasoning.
- Do not claim that Christian prompting has a strong or robust explanation advantage.
- Do not claim that the 7B first-pass heart effect is large or stable across model sizes.
- Do not claim that lexical-echo-controlled explanation results show a confirmed Christian-specific semantic advantage.
- Do not claim that `J1→J2` revision demonstrates strong downstream judgment rewriting.
- Do not claim that the exploratory heterogeneity analysis confirms category-level effects.
- Do not claim that the current results establish final human-coded judgment–explanation consistency.
- Do not imply that “intuition” is an ontological property of the model rather than an operational measurement layer.
